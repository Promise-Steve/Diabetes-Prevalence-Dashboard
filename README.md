## 📌 Project Overview

The Diabetes Prevalence Dashboard is an interactive Microsoft Excel analytics project designed to explore diabetes prevalence, progression, and associated risk factors across different population groups. The project transforms raw healthcare data into meaningful insights through interactive visualisations, key performance indicators (KPIs), and dynamic filtering capabilities.

The dashboard examines how demographic characteristics, lifestyle factors, and clinical indicators influence diabetes prevalence and progression, enabling users to identify patterns that can support data-driven healthcare decision-making.

---

## 🎯 Business Problem

Diabetes remains one of the leading chronic health conditions worldwide, contributing significantly to morbidity, mortality, and healthcare costs. Healthcare professionals and policymakers require accessible analytical tools to identify high-risk populations and understand factors associated with diabetes progression.

This project seeks to answer the following questions:

* Which age groups are most affected by diabetes?
* How does physical activity influence diabetes prevalence?
* How do HbA1c and glucose levels vary across diabetes stages?
* What relationship exists between BMI and diabetes progression?

## 🔍 Key Insights

### 1. Diabetes Prevalence Increases with Age

Individuals aged 55 years and above recorded the highest prevalence of diabetes, suggesting that age is a significant risk factor for diabetes occurrence.

### 2. Physical Inactivity Is Associated with Higher Diabetes Prevalence

The analysis indicates that individuals with lower physical activity levels experience higher rates of diabetes compared to physically active individuals.

### 3. Clinical Indicators Worsen with Diabetes Progression

Average HbA1c and fasting glucose levels increase progressively across the stages of diabetes, reflecting declining glycemic control.

### 4. Overweight and Obesity Are Strongly Associated with Diabetes

Overweight and obese individuals are disproportionately represented within advanced diabetes categories, reinforcing the relationship between BMI and diabetes risk.

---

## 📋 Recommendations

* Promote regular physical activity through targeted public health campaigns.
* Encourage routine diabetes screening among older adults.
* Strengthen obesity prevention and weight management interventions.
* Improve early identification and monitoring of pre-diabetic individuals.
